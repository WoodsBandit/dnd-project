# Chapter 6 - The Thessalhydra's Lair

**Location:** The Thessalhydra's Lair (7 Areas)
**Estimated Time:** 45 minutes - 1 hour
**Type:** Final Boss / Climax

## Recap

The heroes have traveled far: from Sir Tristan's court, through dangerous roads, troglodyte-infested caverns, a cursed labyrinth, and the nightmarish Upside Down. They have faced the Demogorgon and survived. Now, at last, they stand at the entrance to the Thessalhydra's lair.

## Scene Overview

The climactic finale of the adventure. The lair contains 7 areas leading to the final confrontation with the Thessalhydra. A small tribe of troglodyte worshippers guards the creature. Victory means completing the quest and returning to Sir Tristan for rewards.

---

## Key Information

**The Thessalhydra:**
- See `npcs/thessalhydra.md` for full stat block
- CR 7 (2,900 XP)
- 8 heads with regeneration (stopped by fire)
- AC 14, HP 103
- Legendary Actions (2/round)

**Other Threats:**
- Troglodyte Worshippers (small tribe that serves the Thessalhydra)
- Environmental hazards (acid pools, unstable terrain)

**Critical Reminder:** Fire damage prevents head regeneration!

## The 7 Lair Areas

### Area 1: Lair Entrance

> "You emerge from the portal into a cavern that reeks of acid and death. The walls are pitted and scarred, clearly corroded over time. Bones litter the floor - the remains of many meals. A tunnel leads deeper into darkness, and you hear distant hissing."

**Contents:**
- No immediate enemies
- Evidence of the Thessalhydra's presence (acid damage, bones)
- DC 12 Perception: Notice troglodyte tracks leading deeper

**Environmental Hazard:**
- Acid residue on some surfaces (1d4 acid damage if touched)

**Exits:** Tunnel to Area 2

---

### Area 2: Worshipper Outpost

> "The tunnel opens into a small chamber where crude torches provide dim light. Several troglodytes kneel before a crude altar decorated with serpent imagery. They rise as they notice you, hissing with religious fervor."

**Encounter:** 4 Troglodyte Worshippers
- These worship the Thessalhydra as a god
- They fight to the death (no negotiation possible)
- They will raise an alarm if given the chance

**Treasure:**
- Altar offerings: 30 gp, ritual components

**Exits:** To Areas 1, 3, and 4

---

### Area 3: Acid Pool Chamber

> "This chamber is dominated by a bubbling pool of yellowish liquid. The acrid smell burns your eyes and nose. Narrow ledges along the walls provide the only path across."

**Hazard:** Acid Pool
- Falling in: 4d6 acid damage per round
- The acid vapors: DC 12 Constitution save or be Poisoned for 1 minute

**Navigation:**
- DC 12 Acrobatics to cross the ledges safely
- Failure: Fall into acid (4d6 damage) or catch the edge (DC 14 Athletics)

**Treasure:**
- At the bottom of the pool (visible but dangerous): 75 gp in melted coins, a corroded magic ring (Ring of Acid Resistance, ironic)

**Exits:** To Areas 2 and 5

---

### Area 4: The Feeding Chamber

> "The stench here is overwhelming. This is where the creature eats. Half-dissolved carcasses and bones are piled against the walls. Something... partially intact... might still be useful among the remains."

**Contents:**
- No living enemies
- Disturbing evidence of the Thessalhydra's appetite
- Remains of previous adventurers

**Treasure:**
- Searching the remains (DC 14 Investigation):
  - 50 gp in scattered coins
  - A slightly corroded but functional longsword +1
  - 2 Potions of Healing (miraculously intact)
  - A torn journal describing the Thessalhydra's habits

**The Journal Notes:**
> "The creature sleeps in a central chamber. It prefers to attack with its many heads in sequence. Cut the heads faster than they can regrow. Fire - we needed fire..."

**Exits:** To Areas 2 and 5

---

### Area 5: The Inner Sanctum

> "The tunnel widens into a larger passage decorated with crude paintings and carvings - all depicting a many-headed serpent being worshipped by smaller figures. The troglodyte worshippers' holiest ground."

**Encounter:** 3 Troglodyte Zealots + 1 Shaman
- The shaman has spellcasting (Cleric-like abilities)
- They fight fanatically to protect their god

**Shaman Stats (modified Troglodyte):**
- HP 26, AC 12
- Spellcasting (Wisdom, spell save DC 12):
  - Cantrips: Sacred Flame, Guidance
  - 1st Level (3 slots): Healing Word, Shield of Faith, Inflict Wounds

**Treasure:**
- Shaman's staff (worth 25 gp)
- Holy symbol (crude but valuable to collectors, 15 gp)

**Exits:** To Areas 3, 4, 6, and 7

---

### Area 6: Treasury Alcove

> "This small side chamber appears to be where the troglodytes store their most precious offerings to their 'god.' Crude shelves hold glittering objects and strange artifacts."

**Contents:**
- No enemies (if worshippers in Area 5 are defeated)
- The accumulated treasure of the worshippers

**Treasure:**
- 200 gp in coins and gems
- A silver necklace (50 gp)
- A Potion of Fire Resistance (useful for the final fight!)
- A scroll of Fireball (extremely useful!)

**DM Note:** The Fireball scroll and Potion of Fire Resistance are intentionally placed here to help with the final fight. Don't skip this room!

**Exits:** To Area 5

---

### Area 7: The Thessalhydra's Chamber

> "The passage opens into a vast cavern, its ceiling lost in darkness above. Pools of acid dot the floor, and the walls are scarred by countless claw marks and acid burns. At the center, coiled around a pile of bones and treasure, lies your quarry: the Thessalhydra. Eight serpentine heads rise from its massive body, each pair of eyes fixing on you with predatory hunger. The hunt ends here."

**The Final Battle**

**See `npcs/thessalhydra.md` for full stat block.**

**Quick Reference:**
- AC 14, HP 103, Speed 30 ft., swim 30 ft.
- **8 Heads:** Advantage on saves vs. multiple conditions, regenerates heads
- **Multiattack:** Maw + Flurry of Bites
- **Maw:** +7, 1d10+4 piercing + 1d10 acid
- **Flurry of Bites:** +7, 1d6+4 piercing + 4d4 poison
- **Tail Pincer:** +7, 1d12+4 slashing + grapple (DC 14)
- **Acid Saliva (Recharge 5-6):** 4d8 acid, DC 15 DEX, 30 ft. range
- **Legendary Actions (2):** Detect, Tail Swipe

**Key Mechanic - Head Regeneration:**
- 10+ damage in one turn kills a head
- At end of turn, grows 2 heads for each one lost (unless fire damage taken)
- Regains 10 HP per head regrown
- If all 8 heads die, the Thessalhydra dies

**Environmental Features:**

| Feature | Location | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Acid Pools | Floor | 2d6 acid damage if entered |
| Bone Piles | Scattered | Difficult terrain |
| Stalactites | Ceiling | Can be dropped (DC 15 ranged, 2d6 damage) |
| Narrow Passages | Edges | Can limit the creature's movement |

**Lair Actions (Optional, on Initiative 20):**
- Acid spray from walls (DC 13 DEX, 1d6 acid damage)
- Stalactites shake loose (DC 12 DEX, 1d6 bludgeoning)
- Toxic fumes (DC 11 CON or poisoned until end of next turn)

## Combat Tactics

### Thessalhydra Strategy

1. **Opening:** Uses Acid Saliva on grouped enemies
2. **Focus Fire:** Targets wounded enemies with Flurry of Bites
3. **Grapple:** Uses Tail Pincer on the biggest threat (spellcasters, healers)
4. **Legendary Actions:** Detect to find hiding targets, Tail Swipe to control battlefield
5. **Regeneration:** Takes advantage of multiple heads to outlast the party

### Party Strategy Tips

1. **Fire is Essential:** Prevents head regeneration - without it, the fight is nearly unwinnable
2. **Concentrated Damage:** 10+ damage kills a head - coordinate attacks
3. **Spread Out:** Acid Saliva is an AoE - don't cluster
4. **Free the Grappled:** The Tail Pincer grapple can be deadly
5. **Winter's Dark Bite:** +3 bonus against the Thessalhydra specifically

## Victory

When the Thessalhydra is slain:

> "The final head falls, severed by your blade. The great body shudders once, twice, then collapses in a heap of scales and ichor. The Thessalhydra, terror of Crabapple Valley, is dead. For a moment, silence. Then the realization: you have done it. The quest is complete."

**Collecting the Heads:**

## The Epilogue

### Returning to Sir Tristan

> "The journey back is easier than the journey there. The troglodyte caverns are quiet - perhaps word has spread of your victory. The Cursed Labyrinth is no longer cursed, its passages now stable. And the road home is filled with travelers who have heard rumors of heroes."

**At Sir Tristan's Court:**

> "Sir Tristan rises from his throne as you enter, his eyes widening at the sight of the Thessalhydra's heads. 'By the gods,' he breathes. 'You did it. You actually did it.' He claps his hands, and servants rush forward with chests of gold. 'Heroes of Crabapple Valley - you shall be remembered forever!'"

| Reward | Amount |
|--------|--------|

## Alternative Outcomes

### If the Party Retreats

If the party flees the Thessalhydra fight:
- They can return later, better prepared
- The creature heals but doesn't pursue beyond its lair
- Sir Tristan is disappointed but understanding
- Reduced reward for partial success

### If TPK Occurs

- Consider having the party wake up, barely alive, rescued by the troglodyte king (if allied) or the Proud Princess
- They can attempt the fight again with disadvantages
- Or... the Thessalhydra wins, and the story becomes one of tragedy

## DM Notes

### Making the Finale Memorable

- This is the payoff for the entire adventure
- Describe the Thessalhydra as terrifying and impressive
- Give each player heroic moments
- Make victory feel earned

### Running the Combat

- Track heads carefully (use tokens or a counter)
- Remind players about fire damage if they forget
- Use the environment (acid pools, stalactites)
- The fight should be challenging but winnable
- If it's too easy, use lair actions more aggressively
- If it's too hard, have some heads already damaged

### After Victory

- Let players enjoy their triumph
- The return journey can be quick (montage)
- The reward scene should feel satisfying
- Close character arcs if possible
