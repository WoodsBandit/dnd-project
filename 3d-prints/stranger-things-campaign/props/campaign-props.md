# Stranger Things Campaign - Props & Equipment Design Specifications

## 3D Printing Prop Guide for Tabletop Gaming

**Document Purpose:** Comprehensive design specifications for all props, equipment, magic items, and scenic bases featured in the Stranger Things D&D campaign.

**Scale:** 28mm/32mm heroic scale (1 inch = 5 feet game scale)

---

## PRINTING TECHNOLOGY RECOMMENDATIONS

### Resin Printing (SLA/DLP/MSLA) - PREFERRED FOR MOST PROPS
- **Best for:** Fine details, tiny items, jewelry, weapons, intricate textures
- **Layer height:** 0.025mm - 0.05mm for maximum detail
- **Recommended resins:** ABS-like grey, water-washable, or specialty casting resins
- **Post-processing:** Wash, cure, prime with airbrush primer

### FDM Printing - SUITABLE FOR LARGER PIECES
- **Best for:** Furniture, large terrain, scenic bases, structural pieces
- **Layer height:** 0.12mm - 0.16mm
- **Nozzle:** 0.4mm standard, 0.25mm for finer detail
- **Materials:** PLA+ or PETG recommended

## SECTION 1: FURNITURE & LARGE PROPS

### 1.1 Sir Tristan's Throne

**Campaign Context:** The ornate seat of the lost knight Sir Tristan, discovered in the throne room. Symbol of his fallen nobility and the party's ultimate goal.

**Dimensions:**
- Overall: 2" wide x 2" deep x 3" tall (50mm x 50mm x 76mm)
- Seat height: 1" (25mm) - sized to hold a standard 25mm base mini
- Armrest width: 0.25" (6mm) each side
- Back height from seat: 2" (50mm)

**Design Elements:**
- High-backed gothic style with pointed arch top
- Carved heraldic crest on backrest (lion rampant or custom family sigil)
- Armrests carved as lion heads or griffon claws
- Velvet cushion texture on seat and back
- Ornate legs with claw feet
- Decorative scrollwork along frame edges
- Worn edges to suggest age and abandonment

**Structural Features:**
- Hollow backrest to reduce material and print time
- Reinforced armrests (minimum 2mm thickness)
- Flat underside for stability
- Optional: separate cushion piece for painting contrast

**Print Recommendations:**
- **Method:** FDM acceptable, resin for maximum detail
- **Orientation:** Print upside down for best seat surface
- **Supports:** Required for armrests and decorative elements
- **Infill:** 15-20% for FDM
- **Print time:** 4-6 hours FDM, 8-10 hours resin

**Painting Guide:**
- Base: Dark wood grain (Vallejo Charred Brown)
- Drybrush: Lighter wood tones
- Cushion: Royal purple or crimson velvet
- Gold leaf accents on carvings
- Weathering: Dust accumulation in crevices

### 1.2 Treasure Chest

**Campaign Context:** Classic dungeon loot container, discovered in the Troglodyte lair and Thessalhydra hoard.

**Dimensions:**
- Closed: 1" wide x 0.75" deep x 0.5" tall (25mm x 19mm x 13mm)
- Open: Same base, lid extends to 0.75" height when open
- Interior depth: 0.3" (8mm)

**Version A - Closed Chest:**
- Domed lid with reinforced metal bands
- Heavy iron lock plate on front
- Corner brackets with rivets
- Rope handles on sides
- Wood plank texture on body
- Iron hinges visible on back

**Version B - Open Chest (Revealing Treasure):**
- Lid hinged open at 110-degree angle
- Interior contents modeled:
  - Pile of gold coins (raised bumpy texture)
  - 2-3 visible gems on top
  - Rolled scroll or parchment edge visible
  - Single chalice or crown partially visible
- Lid interior shows iron reinforcement pattern

**Version C - Empty Open Chest:**
- Same as Version B but empty interior
- Interior wood grain texture visible
- Useful for looted or trapped chest scenarios

**Structural Features:**
- Flat bottom with slight feet at corners
- Lid can be printed separately for hinge assembly (optional)
- Single-piece versions are more durable

**Print Recommendations:**
- **Method:** Resin preferred for crisp wood grain and treasure detail
- **Orientation:** Print right-side up
- **Supports:** Minimal, light supports for lid overhang on open version
- **Layer height:** 0.03mm resin
- **Print time:** 45 minutes - 1 hour per chest

**Painting Guide:**
- Wood: Mahogany or oak tones with grain highlighting
- Metal bands: Weathered iron or bronze
- Lock: Brass with verdigris
- Gold coins: Retributor Armour with Agrax wash
- Gems: Colored glazes over silver base

## SECTION 2: MAGIC ITEMS (Display Scale)

### 2.1 Winter's Dark Bite - Greatsword

**Campaign Context:** Sir Tristan's legendary weapon, a greatsword of black metal that seems to absorb light. The ultimate treasure of the campaign.

**Dimensions:**
- Total length: 3" (76mm) display scale
- Blade length: 2.25" (57mm)
- Blade width at base: 0.25" (6mm)
- Grip length: 0.5" (13mm)
- Crossguard width: 0.6" (15mm)

**Design Elements:**

*Blade:*
- Straight, double-edged greatsword profile
- Fuller (blood groove) running 2/3 of blade length
- Subtle frost patterns etched near edges
- Blade appears to have depth - design inset runes or void patterns
- Tip slightly curved for piercing

*Crossguard:*
- Swept forward slightly in aggressive stance
- Ends taper to sharp points
- Engraved with winter motifs (snowflakes, frost crystals)
- Center boss with embedded dark gem

*Grip:*
- Wrapped in leather cord texture
- Ergonomic finger grooves
- Slight taper toward pommel

*Pommel:*
- Heavy counterweight design
- Cracked ice/crystal pattern
- Small dark gem set in center
- Can be used as bludgeon

**Display Options:**
1. **Wall mount:** Small bracket to hang sword horizontally
2. **Angled stand:** 45-degree display cradle
3. **Vertical stand:** Pommel-down with blade rising
4. **Ice block base:** Sword emerging from frozen chunk

**Print Recommendations:**
- **Method:** RESIN ONLY - FDM cannot capture the detail
- **Orientation:** Flat on build plate with blade parallel
- **Supports:** Light supports on crossguard only
- **Layer height:** 0.025mm for rune detail
- **Material:** Standard grey or black resin
- **Print time:** 2-3 hours

**Painting Guide:**
- Prime black, NOT grey
- Blade: Gloss black with subtle dark blue edge highlighting
- Do NOT drybrush - maintain light-absorbing appearance
- Frost effects: Very subtle ice blue glazes in runes only
- Metal parts: Dark gunmetal, almost black
- Gems: Deep purple or black with gloss finish
- Grip: Matte dark leather brown

**Special Effect:**
- Consider gloss varnish on blade only for "liquid darkness" effect
- Matte varnish on handle for contrast

### 3.1 Potions of Healing (x5)

**Campaign Context:** Standard healing potions, the party's lifeline in dangerous encounters.

**Dimensions (each):**
- Height: 0.3" (8mm) including cork
- Bottle body height: 0.25" (6mm)
- Bottle diameter: 0.12" (3mm)

**Design Variations (5 unique bottles):**

1. **Round Flask:** Classic spherical body with short neck
2. **Teardrop:** Elongated drop shape, wider at bottom
3. **Square Bottle:** Geometric apothecary style
4. **Spiral Vial:** Twisted glass pattern
5. **Heart Bottle:** Subtle heart shape (symbolizing health)

**Design Elements:**
- Clear/translucent appearance (if using clear resin)
- Visible liquid line at 3/4 full
- Cork stopper with wax seal
- Small label or tied tag
- Bubbles visible in liquid (tiny spherical indents)

**Print Recommendations:**
- **Method:** RESIN ONLY - clear or translucent red resin
- **Layer height:** 0.025mm
- **Print time:** 20-30 minutes for set
- **Material option:** Print hollow with drainage hole, fill with UV resin for liquid effect

**Painting Guide:**
- If solid resin: Glaze with Blood Angels Red contrast
- Cork: Zandri Dust, Ushabti Bone drybrush
- Wax seal: Darker red or black
- Label: Cream with brown ink text suggestion
- Highlight: White reflection spot on glass

**Display Suggestion:**
- Small apothecary shelf or potion rack
- Fits 5-10 potions in row
- Include tiny bottles on rack
